# Product Brief

## Working concept

The MTG Card Info App is a mobile application where each Magic: The Gathering card has a dedicated reference and discussion page.

A user should be able to search for a card, review its general card information, move between its different printings, and read or join conversations tied either to the card as a whole or to one specific printing.

The goal is not to replace every existing Magic app. The goal is to organize card-specific knowledge and discussion better than a general forum, social feed, or chat server.

## The problem

Useful information about individual cards is scattered across Reddit, Discord, YouTube, marketplace listings, deck-building sites, old forum posts, and private collector groups.

A collector trying to identify a printing may have a different question from a player researching a rules interaction, but both may be searching for the same card. Existing tools often provide the official card text and a market price, then leave the user to search elsewhere for context.

Common questions include:

- Why is one printing worth more than another?
- Is a particular foil treatment known to curl?
- Is this defect normal for the set?
- How can this printing be distinguished from a counterfeit?
- Does a grading company tend to treat this release differently?
- What decks or formats use this card?
- How does this card interact with another rule or effect?

The answers often exist, but they are difficult to find again because they were posted inside a broad community rather than attached to the card itself.

## Initial audience

The first audience is an engaged Magic player or collector who regularly looks up cards and wants more than rules text and a current listing price.

Likely users include:

- Collectors comparing printings, finishes, condition, and value
- Players researching rules, interactions, and practical card use
- Buyers investigating a card before making a purchase
- Sellers trying to identify a version or describe a condition issue
- Experienced community members who enjoy sharing specialized knowledge

## Core value

Search for a card and go directly to the information and conversation about that card.

The most important structural distinction is between information that belongs to the card generally and information that belongs to one physical printing.

For example, a rules question about *Lightning Bolt* applies across printings. A question about Alpha print quality, Revised coloration, or a Secret Lair foil applies to a particular printing.

## Minimum viable product

The first usable version should include:

1. User accounts and basic profiles
2. A searchable Magic card database
3. A dedicated page for each card
4. A way to browse and select individual printings
5. General card information and official rules text
6. Printing-specific information
7. An all-printings discussion feed
8. A selected-printing discussion feed
9. Posts, replies, and basic voting or usefulness signals
10. Topic categories and filters
11. Saved cards or followed discussions
12. Notifications for followed activity
13. Reporting and basic moderation tools

## Discussion categories

All-printings discussions may include:

- Gameplay
- Rules and interactions
- Deckbuilding
- Combos and synergies
- Card history and artwork
- Reprint discussion
- General questions

Printing-specific discussions may include:

- Pricing and collecting
- Condition and grading
- Identification and counterfeits
- Print quality and defects
- Foiling and curling
- Availability and distribution
- Other printing-specific topics

These should be post categories and filters, not separate subforums. A single feed is less likely to feel empty and is easier to browse on a phone.

## Not part of the first version

The first release is not planned to include:

- A card marketplace
- Direct sales or payment processing
- Private trading
- Full collection management
- Deck building
- Automated grading
- AI-generated valuations
- Live auction aggregation
- Support for every trading card game
- A short-form video or general social-media feed

Some of these may be useful later, but adding them immediately would blur the purpose of the app and create unnecessary competition with established products.

## Main product risk

The largest risk is the empty-page problem.

Magic has tens of thousands of cards and many more individual printings. Most pages will not naturally attract the same level of activity. An app filled with empty discussions will feel unfinished even if the card database works correctly.

The early product should therefore concentrate activity around:

- Popular cards
- Newly released sets
- High-value cards
- Frequently searched cards
- Cards with known printing or identification issues
- Cards involved in current rules, format, or reprint discussions

Curated starter content may be necessary before a public launch.

## Questions that still need testing

Before substantial development begins, the project should answer:

1. Do players and collectors regularly search for card-specific information that current apps do not provide?
2. Would they install a separate app for this purpose?
3. Which questions create repeat use instead of one-time visits?
4. Will knowledgeable users contribute answers?
5. Is the distinction between all-printings and printing-specific discussion immediately understandable?
6. Can the app be useful before it has a large community?
7. What would make a user open this app before searching Google, Reddit, or Discord?

## Early signs of success

Interest alone is not enough to validate the idea. Better evidence would include:

- Users returning several times in a month
- Users following multiple cards or discussions
- Questions receiving useful answers
- Experienced users contributing without being prompted
- Card pages being shared outside the app
- Search traffic reaching useful public card discussions
- A meaningful percentage of users posting, replying, voting, or saving content

## Immediate objective

Create a clickable mobile prototype that includes:

1. Home and search
2. Search results
3. The card page
4. The printing selector
5. All-printings discussion
6. This-printing discussion
7. A discussion thread
8. The process for creating a post

The prototype should be tested with actual Magic players and collectors before a technology stack is finalized.
